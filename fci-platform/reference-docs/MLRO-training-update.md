Priority: Medium Documents affected: mlro-escalation-decisions.md

What the training states: All EDD and high-risk profile escalations are handled by the High-Risk Customer (HRC) investigations unit. PEP escalations go directly to the ADGM MLRO — not via HRC.

"All EDD and high-risk profile users are referred to the High-Risk Customer (HRC) investigations unit for handling. PEP escalations are sent directly to the ADGM MLRO / Deputy MLRO without routing through HRC first."

Priority: Low Documents affected: mlro-escalation-decisions.md, icr-steps-decision.md

Current knowledge base text: The 35-day SLA is documented but is presented as a single undivided figure. The two-stage structure is not explained. Investigators reading the current documentation cannot determine how the 35 days are allocated between the investigation phase and the reporting phase.

Recommended addition to mlro-escalation-decisions.md ADGM Special Requirements:

"The 35-day general SLA comprises two sequential stages: alert investigation must be completed within 30 days of alert generation; subsequent external reporting to the FIU via the goAML portal must be completed within a further 5 days. Priority: High Documents affected: icr-steps-decision.md

What the training states: All .com users now fall under ADGM as a licensed entity from 5 January 2026. The previous concept of "non-reporting jurisdictions" for .com users is no longer applicable in the same way — ADGM is the regulated home for all .com users not covered by a specific local jurisdiction MLRO. In reality, the full ADGM table applies from 5 January 2026, and many additional case types (money laundering, fraud, market abuse, etc.) do require ADGM escalation when the risk threshold is met.

Recommended replacement text for icr-steps-decision.md Step 21 — ADGM Quick Reference section:

The ADGM table applies a conditional escalation standard to most case types: escalation is required where suspicious money laundering, terrorist financing, or other criminal activity is determined. Always verify the specific case type against the ADGM table before concluding no escalation is required."
