# Crypto Gambling Regulation by Country

## Afghanistan
- **Status:** Illegal: explicitly banned
- **Notes:** Cryptocurrency is illegal in Afghanistan - the Central bank has imposed a nationwide ban on cryptocurrencies and the Taliban regime has been arresting traders periodically who refuse to comply [1].

## Albania
- **Status:** Illegal: explicitly banned [1]

## Algeria
- **Status:** Illegal: explicitly banned
- **Notes:** Algeria currently prohibits the use of cryptocurrency following the passing of the 2018 Financial Law that made it illegal to buy, sell, use or hold virtual currencies [1].

## American Samoa
- **Status:** Illegal: explicitly banned
- **Notes:** Same as United States [1].

## Andorra
- **Status:** Unregulated/unknown
- **Notes:** Gambling is regulated in Andorra and Binance does not have the license to promote gambling or offer it. Do not promote or offer any gambling campaign to the AD market [1].

## Angola
- **Status:** Conditionally legal: regulated and legal, but only if licensed locally
- **Notes:** Although crypto itself is unregulated, crypto could constitute online gambling, which is generally regulated [1].

## Anguilla
- **Status:** Unregulated/unknown
- **Notes:** Same as United Kingdom: Buying or selling cryptocurrencies is not classified as gambling by the UK's Gambling Act [1].

## Antarctica
- **Status:** Other: unclear/special
- **Notes:** Do we have any Antarctica-resident client? [1]

## Antigua and Barbuda
- **Status:** Conditionally legal: regulated and legal, but only if licensed locally [1]

## Argentina
- **Status:** Conditionally legal: regulated and legal, but only if licensed locally
- **Notes:** Crypto gambling would constitute online gambling, which is regulated and requires license. Licensed online gambling sites in ARG have a "bet.ar" domain [1].

## Armenia
- **Status:** Unregulated/unknown
- **Notes:** Online gambling is legal in the Republic of Armenia, according to the laws of RA and Decree 1164-N of the GoA on gambling. Despite offering legal online and land-based gambling, Armenia has adopted an anti-gambling policy towards advertisements for betting. In terms of cryptocurrencies and digital assets, there is no existing legislative framework on crypto in the country [1].

## Aruba
- **Status:** Conditionally legal: regulated and legal, but only if licensed locally
- **Notes:** Same as Netherlands: Gambling in the Netherlands is regulated under the Betting and Gambling Act and Betting and Gambling Tax Act. It is prohibited to offer games of chance without the requisite licence, facilitate or promote unlicensed games of chance, and knowingly participate in unlicensed games of chance [1].

## Australia
- **Status:** Conditionally legal: regulated and legal, but only if licensed locally
- **Notes:** A gambling licence is required in each state for gambling - we do not have a gambling license so such activities are not permitted [1].

## Austria
- **Status:** Conditionally legal: regulated and legal, but only if State-owned
- **Notes:** Regulation for online gambling regulation will apply regardless if there is crypto assets involved or not. In case crypto assets are subject to gambling, rewards, incentives, etc. gambling provider, supporter must obtain a registration/license to provide crypto asset services [1].

## Azerbaijan
- **Status:** Illegal: explicitly banned
- **Notes:** Azerbaijan has held a general prohibition on gambling since 1998. In 2011, the country decided to approve sports betting. The only legal operator in the country is eTopaz. Online casinos remained outlawed [1].

## Bahamas
- **Status:** Conditionally legal: regulated and legal, but only if licensed locally
- **Notes:** No crypto gambling specific regulation. Laws relating to gambling would apply [1].

## Bahrain
- **Status:** Other: unclear/special
- **Notes:** The law says that this is illegal in public places. This topic is highly controversial and the practice locally is to inform FID of gambling activities carried out by the users in case suspicious behaviour is picked up. Accordingly banks and other FIs practice is to be prudent and only allow their users transact with licensed entities [1].

## Bangladesh
- **Status:** Illegal: explicitly banned
- **Notes:** In Bangladesh, cryptocurrencies are also banned as it goes against the country's financial regulations. Bangladesh Bank does not allow holding or trading of any kind of virtual coin or cryptocurrency in the country. If found violating the laws, crypto traders can face imprisonment [1].
